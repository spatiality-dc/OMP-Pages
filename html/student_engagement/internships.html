Monash postgraduate students have the unique opportunity to obtain academic credit while gaining practical experience in the aid and development sector by undertaking an internship with Oxfam Australia.

We offer various internships each year through the Oxfam-Monash Partnership and at Oxfam head office to students looking to learn first-hand about the work of the development sector, contribute their own fresh ideas, energy and skills, and become inspired future leaders of positive global change.

Internships are offered through the Arts Workplace Integrated Learning (WIL) area and may be undertaken in areas such as gender equality, humanitarian response, financial inclusion and supporting technologies.

See below for current internship opportunities available to students undertaking studies in International Development or related areas.

For further information about internships email us: MSDI-OxfamMonashPartnership@monash.edu

Internship Lengths

Variable depending on project requirements. Generally they run over the course of a semester, although they may be conducted more intensively or over a longer-time frame – see specific internship for details.

Academic unit timeframe requirements still apply – see WIL information.

Eligibility

To access APG5044 Professional Internship, you need:

  • A distinction average (WAM 70) across 24 credit points of core coursework units.
  • Approval to undertake an internship from your Director of Program. You need to discuss your internship intentions and be advised as to whether the internship meets the requirements of your degree.

Application Process and Requirements

Information about application process and required documentation can be found on the Arts WIL page: http://artsonline.monash.edu.au/internships/graduate/how-to-apply-australian-internships/

If you have any questions about the application process, please contact the Arts WIL Team via email wil.arts@monash.edu or phone (03) 9903 8866.

Student Testimonials

Courtney May interned with the Oxfam-Monash Partnership in the second half of 2017 as part of her Masters degree program. Currently completing the final stages of her Masters in Environmental Management and Sustainability, she went on to secure ongoing employment with OMP upon completion of her internship as well as in the Supporter Services area of Oxfam Australia, whilst continuing to work with Monash academics in drafting academic discussion pieces around the workshop themes.

Through her studies and volunteering interests more broadly, Courtney developed a keen interest in issues around environmental governance, climate change and resilience, and gender rights, discovering a distinct passion for understanding the effects of climate change on marginalised groups, particularly women. This led her to apply for the OMP internship during which time she assisted in the organisation of a workshop aiming to explore contemporary issues relating to the effects of microfinance on women’s empowerment and poverty alleviation, focusing on the Asia Pacific region.

“This internship was such an exciting opportunity to work closely with academia to attain a much deeper understanding of a contemporary development issue - without having to write a thesis - whilst simultaneously gaining more practical experience in this space. It was so eye-opening to be in the room for such an amazing discussion between internationally-renowned academics and development practitioners, and to go on to work further with the Oxfam-Monash Partnership is amazing.

I’m now looking forward to working alongside the Program Lead in preparing a joint Oxfam-Monash position paper on microfinance and gender empowerment based on expert recommendations raised at the workshop, as well as having the opportunity to improve our social media and broader online presence.”
