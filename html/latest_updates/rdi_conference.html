Leadership for Inclusive Development

The Oxfam-Monash Partnership was again delighted to take part in the Research for Development Impact (RDI) Network's annual international conference, held in July 2019 at La Trobe University.


An integral part of the international development calendar, RDI Conferences are a flagship series of events running since 2009, giving practitioners, academics, students, consultants and private sector representatives a unique opportunity to hear from thought leaders, interrogate ideas, share practice and explore collaboration across sectors.

About the conference

This year's Conference explored the leadership required 'to leave no-one behind', as an underlying principle of the Sustainable Development Goals. Discussions focussed on how leadership can and does advance inclusion and social change, as well as addressing not just the technical challenges of development but also the power, political, social and cultural dimensions that impact experiences of inclusion or exclusion.

An exciting line-up of speakers offered diverse perspectives on the theme, including the Hon Fiame Mata'afa (Deputy Prime Minister, Samoa) who explored her experience as female leader and the broader challenges facing our region in a changing world and in the face of challenges such as climate change; Srilatha Batliwala (Senior Advisor, Knowledge Building and Feminist Leadership, CREA) who discussed the concept and practice of transformative feminist leadership; and Dan Honig (Assistant Professor of International Development, Johns Hopkins School of Advanced International Studies) examined the ways in which donor practice can better support local leadership.

Oxfam-Monash Partnership presentation

Oxfam-Monash Partnership was also excited to be present in more informal sessions presented staff from Oxfam Australia and various country offices, including sessions on how programs such as She Can Lead are helping build enabling spaces in Pakistan for women's individual to collective action, and the inclusion of women's voices and leadership in Mekong water. We also welcomed sessions by Monash University academics in areas including paradoxical leadership and public sector innovation.

Watch videos of the keynote sessions (including the Q&A) on RDI's YouTube channel. Papers from some of the speakers were also shared in the November edition of the Development Bulletin, which investigated the types of leadership that either encourage or exclude the opportunities for community involvement in planning and implementing beneficial change.
